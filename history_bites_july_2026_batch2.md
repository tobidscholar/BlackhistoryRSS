**July 14, 2026**

Did you know that today is George Washington Carver National Monument Day?

On this day in 1943, Congress and President Franklin Roosevelt established the George Washington Carver National Monument in Diamond, Missouri, the first National Park Service site honoring an African American and the first honoring anyone besides a president.

National Park Service

**July 15, 2026**

Did you know that today is Maggie Lena Walker Day?

On this day in 1864, Maggie Lena Walker was born in Richmond, Virginia; in 1903 she became the first woman of any race to charter and lead a U.S. bank, building Black economic self-reliance through the St. Luke Penny Savings Bank.

National Park Service, Maggie L. Walker National Historic Site

**July 16, 2026**

Did you know that today is Ida B. Wells Day?

On this day in 1862, Ida B. Wells was born in Holly Springs, Mississippi; she became the nation's foremost anti-lynching investigator, using data-driven journalism to expose racial terror even after a mob burned down her newspaper office.

Ida B. Wells Museum, Holly Springs

<facts outlived the flames>

**July 17, 2026**

Did you know that today is Militia Act Day?

On this day in 1862, President Lincoln signed the Militia Act, formally authorizing the U.S. military to enlist Black men as soldiers and laborers for the first time, opening the path nearly 200,000 African Americans would follow into Union service.

National Archives

**July 18, 2026**

Did you know that today is Nelson Mandela Day?

On this day in 1918, Nelson Mandela was born in Mvezo, South Africa; his decades of anti-apartheid leadership, imprisonment, and eventual presidency made July 18 an official United Nations day honoring service to humanity.

Nelson Mandela Foundation

**July 19, 2026**

Did you know that today is Seneca Falls Convention Day?

On this day in 1848, the Seneca Falls Convention opened in New York, where Frederick Douglass argued forcefully for women's suffrage, helping sway delegates to adopt a resolution demanding the vote for women alongside the Declaration of Sentiments.

National Park Service, Women's Rights National Historical Park

Did you know that today is Black Power Conference Day?

On this day in 1967, more than 1,000 delegates from over 100 organizations gathered in Newark, New Jersey, for the first National Black Power Conference, marking a decisive turn from integrationist strategy toward Black self-determination.

Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture

**July 21, 2026**

Did you know that today is New Orleans Tribune Day?

On this day in 1864, Dr. Louis Charles Roudanez launched the New Orleans Tribune, the first Black daily newspaper in the United States, publishing in English and French to advocate for Reconstruction-era civil and voting rights.

Amistad Research Center, Tulane University

Did you know that today is Emancipation Proclamation Preview Day?

On this day in 1862, President Lincoln informed his cabinet he intended to issue a proclamation freeing enslaved people in Confederate territory, though he waited for a Union military victory before announcing it publicly that September.

Library of Congress

**July 24, 2026**

Did you know that today is Alexandre Dumas Day?

On this day in 1802, Alexandre Dumas was born near Paris to a French marquis and Marie-Cessette Dumas, a formerly enslaved Haitian woman; he became France's most prolific novelist, author of *The Three Musketeers* and *The Count of Monte Cristo*.

**July 25, 2026**

Did you know that today is Tuskegee Study Exposure Day?

On this day in 1972, Associated Press reporter Jean Heller exposed the U.S. Public Health Service's 40-year Tuskegee Syphilis Study, revealing that hundreds of Black men had been deliberately denied treatment, a scandal that reshaped American medical ethics.

National Archives

**July 26, 2026**

Did you know that today is Liberian Independence Day?

On this day in 1847, Liberia declared itself an independent republic under President Joseph Jenkins Roberts, becoming Africa's first modern republic, founded largely by freeborn and formerly enslaved Black Americans who resettled on the West African coast.

Liberian Ministry of Information

**July 27, 2026**

Did you know that today is Chicago Race Riot Day?

On this day in 1919, the drowning of seventeen-year-old Eugene Williams at a segregated Lake Michigan beach ignited a week of racial violence on Chicago's South Side, one of the deadliest episodes of the nationwide "Red Summer."

Chicago History Museum

Did you know that today is Fourteenth Amendment Day?

On this day in 1868, Secretary of State William Seward certified the Fourteenth Amendment's ratification, constitutionally guaranteeing birthright citizenship and equal protection under the law to formerly enslaved African Americans.

National Archives

On this day in 1895, Black women's clubs convened in Boston for the First National Conference of Colored Women, organized by Josephine St. Pierre Ruffin, laying groundwork for the National Association of Colored Women founded the following year.

National Association of Colored Women's Clubs

**July 30, 2026**

Did you know that today is New Orleans Massacre Day?

On this day in 1866, a white mob attacked a Republican political convention supporting Black suffrage in New Orleans, killing dozens of African Americans and their allies, a massacre that helped justify federal Reconstruction enforcement in the South.

Library of Congress

On this day in 1874, Patrick Francis Healy, born enslaved in Georgia, was inaugurated president of Georgetown University, becoming the first person of African descent to lead a predominantly white American university.

Georgetown University Archives
